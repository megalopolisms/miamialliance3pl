Supreme Court Rules Trump Tariffs Illegal — Billions in Refunds at Stake for Importers

The U.S. Supreme Court issued a landmark ruling finding that President Trump's use of tariff authority under IEEPA was illegal, opening the door for importers to seek billions in duty refunds. Companies that stockpiled imported goods to front-run tariffs now face a dramatically changed cost landscape. The ruling has massive implications for logistics, warehousing, and the entire import supply chain.

Source: CNBC
Freight Feb 16, 2026

Freight Market Update: Reefer Rejections Surge to 22.5%, Capacity Remains Fragile

National van tender rejection rate held at 13.43% while reefer rejections surged to 22.5% — up 4.05% month over month — and flatbed rejections hit 24.8%. Southeast hubs like Atlanta are experiencing firmer conditions. Domestic loaded intermodal volume increased 5.69% year over year as shippers seek alternatives to tight truck capacity.

Source: Megacorp Logistics

Trucking Conditions Hit Strongest Level Since February 2022

The FTR Trucking Conditions Index shows market conditions have reached their best performance in approximately four years. After a prolonged freight downturn through most of 2025, the market is showing durable improvement driven by stronger freight rates and better capacity utilization — signaling a more favorable environment for U.S. carriers heading into spring.

Source: FTR Intelligence
Ports Feb 9, 2026

Port Tracker Projects U.S. Import Declines for First Half of 2026

The NRF's Port Tracker report projects 12.27 million TEU across 11 major U.S. ports — including Miami and Port Everglades — representing a 2% annual decrease. Full-year 2026 imports are projected down 2.3%. NRF VP Jonathan Gold: "With tariffs still a matter of debate in the courts, their effect on imports is being clearly seen."

Source: Logistics Management / NRF

Supply Chain Bankruptcies Mount — Las Vegas 3PL Among Latest Filings

Eight more companies filed for Chapter 11 protection in the latest wave of supply chain bankruptcies. Global Logistics and Fulfillment LLC, a Las Vegas-based 3PL/warehousing company with 50-200 employees, filed with $1.5M in creditor claims. Drayage carriers and truck repair shops also filed, reflecting persistent financial pressure from excess capacity and soft spot rates.

Source: FreightWaves

Florida Industrial Warehouse Market: Tampa Dominates, South Florida Adds 485K SF

Florida cements itself as a national logistics powerhouse. Tampa leads with five consecutive years of 10M+ SF annual absorption and just 3.2% vacancy for small bays. South Florida's Festival Logistics Park in Pompano Beach is delivering 485,000 SF of new Class A industrial space. PortMiami and Port Everglades forecast 4-6% container growth annually through 2028.

February Supply Chain Outlook: Chinese New Year + Ice Storms Converge
