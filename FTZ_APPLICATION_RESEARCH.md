## 1. Executive Summary & Recommended Path

**Bottom Line:** Miami Alliance 3PL **cannot** apply directly as a "grantee" for a new FTZ (that requires a public corporation or special state legislation). Instead, Miami Alliance 3PL should pursue **usage-driven site designation** under the existing **FTZ No. 32** (grantee: Greater Miami Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc., administered by the Greater Miami Chamber of Commerce).

**Why FTZ 32?**

- FTZ 32's service area covers **all of Miami-Dade County** (expanded under ASF in 2020)
- Medley, FL is within Miami-Dade County = within FTZ 32's ASF service area
- The ASF Minor Boundary Modification (MBM) process takes as little as **30 days** for approval
- FTZ 32 already has logistics operators in Medley (e.g., Crowley Logistics at 10205 NW 108th Ave, EELCO at 8503 NW 80th Street)

**Alternative:** FTZ No. 281 (grantee: Miami-Dade County / PortMiami) also covers Miami-Dade County and may be an option, though FTZ 32 is the more established general-purpose zone in the area.

**Estimated total timeline:** 3-9 months (30 days for FTZ Board designation + 3-6 months for CBP activation)
**Estimated total cost:** $10,000-$25,000 (application fees + grantee fees + bond + setup)

## 2. The Foreign-Trade Zones Board

### Composition

The Foreign-Trade Zones Board consists of:

- **Secretary of Commerce** (Chair)
- **Secretary of the Treasury**
- The **Commissioner of U.S. Customs and Border Protection** provides input on customs and security matters during voting

### Authority

- Established by the Foreign-Trade Zones Act of 1934 (19 U.S.C. 81a-81u)
- Grants authority to establish, operate, and maintain foreign-trade zones
- Reviews and approves applications for new zones, subzones, and production authority
- Administers the Alternative Site Framework (ASF)

## 4. Application Types Compared

### Option A: New Zone (Grantee Application)

- **Regulatory Basis:** 15 CFR 400.21
- **Who Applies:** Public corporation (preferred) or private corporation with special state legislation
- **Timeline:** 10+ months after docketing
- **Fee:** $3,200 (Board fee)
- **Verdict for Miami3PL:** **NOT RECOMMENDED** -- Requires public entity or special state legislation; extremely lengthy process; unnecessary when FTZ 32 already covers Medley

### Option B: Subzone Application (Non-ASF)

- **Regulatory Basis:** 15 CFR 400.25
- **Who Applies:** Filed by zone grantee on behalf of operator/company
- **Timeline:** 5 months after docketing (3 months for certain cases under 400.36(f))
- **Fee:** $4,000 (Board fee, no production) or $6,500 (with production of 3+ products)
- **Verdict for Miami3PL:** **VIABLE BUT SLOWER** -- Appropriate if site is outside ASF service area or grantee prefers this route

### Option C: Usage-Driven Site Under ASF (Minor Boundary Modification)

- **Regulatory Basis:** 15 CFR 400.24(c); FTZ Board ASF procedures
- **Who Applies:** Filed by zone grantee (FTZ 32) on behalf of the operator (Miami3PL)
- **Timeline:** As fast as **30 days** from complete application filing
- **Fee:** No Board fee for MBM (grantee fees apply)
- **Verdict for Miami3PL:** **STRONGLY RECOMMENDED** -- Fastest, simplest, cheapest path

### Option D: Magnet Site Operator

- **What:** Operate within an existing, already-activated magnet site
- **Who:** Enter operator agreement with grantee; activate with CBP
- **Timeline:** Depends on grantee; requires CBP activation only
- **Verdict for Miami3PL:** **Only if existing magnet site includes Miami3PL's address** (unlikely for 8780 NW 100th ST)

## 5. Recommended Path: Usage-Driven Site Under FTZ 32 ASF

### Why This Is the Best Path

1. **FTZ 32 has ASF coverage of all Miami-Dade County** (approved May 12, 2020; FTZ Docket B-74-2019; 85 FR 28689)
2. **Medley, FL 33178 is in Miami-Dade County** = within service area
3. **Usage-driven site designation via ASF MBM can be approved in 30 days**
4. **No FTZ Board application fee** for minor boundary modifications
5. **Grantee (Greater Miami Chamber) already manages 106+ active sites** = experienced and streamlined process
6. **Other logistics companies in Medley already operate under FTZ 32** (Crowley Logistics, EELCO)

### What Is a Usage-Driven Site?

A usage-driven site is approved for a **specific company/use** within a zone's ASF service area. It provides FTZ status to your specific warehouse location. Unlike a magnet site (open to multiple operators at a fixed location), a usage-driven site is designated specifically for Miami Alliance 3PL at 8780 NW 100th ST.

- **Port of Entry:** Miami, FL
- **Purpose:** Discuss activation requirements, timeline, and any site-specific concerns
- Discuss inventory control and recordkeeping system requirements
- Learn about background check and security requirements

#### Step 1.4: Determine Activity Scope

Document what activities Miami Alliance 3PL will perform in the FTZ:

**Permitted without production authority:**

- Storage/warehousing
- Distribution
- Assembly
- Cleaning
- Testing
- Repackaging/relabeling
- Repair
- Sampling
- Exhibition
- Manipulation (sorting, grading, etc.)

**Requires separate FTZ Board production authority (15 CFR 400.14(a)):**

- Manufacturing
- Substantial transformation of foreign articles
- Activities resulting in different customs classification

For a 3PL warehouse, most activities will fall under non-production (warehousing, storage, distribution, repackaging). No separate production application will likely be needed.

### PHASE 2: GRANTEE APPLICATION (Weeks 4-8)

#### Step 2.1: Execute Operator Agreement with Grantee

Sign an Operator Agreement with Greater Miami Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc. governing:

- Proposed operations at the site
- Rates and charges
- Annual reporting obligations
- Compliance requirements
- Term and termination
- Insurance requirements
- Public utility obligations (uniform treatment, fair rates per 19 U.S.C. 81n)

#### Step 2.2: Prepare ASF Minor Boundary Modification (MBM) Application

The grantee files this with the FTZ Board. Required information includes:

**Operator/Company Information:**

- Full legal name: Miami Alliance 3PL (or formal corporate name)
- Principal officers/owners
- Corporate structure

**Proposed Site Information:**

- Address: 8780 NW 100th ST, Medley, FL 33178
- Physical size (acreage or square feet)
- Legal description of property (if required)
- Type of designation: Usage-Driven Site

**Activity Description:**

- Nature of operations (warehousing, distribution, logistics)
- Types of merchandise to be handled
- Countries of origin for imports
- Estimated volume of activity

**Maps (per 15 CFR 400.21(d)(5)(ii)):**

- State/county map showing general location relative to transportation network
- Detailed site plan showing zone boundaries in RED, street names, existing/proposed structures
- Relationship to other FTZ 32 sites (if applicable)

**Environmental Statement:**

- Per 15 CFR 400.21(d)(2)(vii), a statement addressing environmental aspects
- For an existing warehouse, this is typically minimal (the building already exists)
- No full NEPA Environmental Impact Statement is expected for a usage-driven site in an existing facility

**CBP Comments:**

- The grantee will request comments from the local CBP port director
- CBP must provide input on the new site for the application to be complete

#### Step 2.3: Grantee Files MBM with FTZ Board

- Grantee submits complete application to ftz@trade.gov
- MBM applications do not require a Board application fee (per 15 CFR 400.29)

### PHASE 3: FTZ BOARD REVIEW (30 Days)

#### Step 3.1: FTZ Board Staff Review

- FTZ Board staff reviews the MBM application
- Standard processing time: **30 days** from receipt of complete application
- If deficiencies are found, applicant has 30 days to correct and resubmit

#### Step 3.2: Approval

- FTZ Board approves the usage-driven site designation
- Designation is subject to:
  - The FTZ Act (19 U.S.C. 81a-81u)
  - FTZ Board regulations (15 CFR Part 400)
  - The Board's standard 2,000-acre activation limit for FTZ 32
  - Specific conditions, if any

### PHASE 4: CBP ACTIVATION (3-6 Months)

#### Step 4.1: Prepare Activation Application

After FTZ Board approval, apply to the **local CBP Port Director** under **19 CFR 146.6**. The application must include:

1. **Written Application** to the Port Director describing:
   - All zone sites covered
   - Operations to be conducted
   - General character of merchandise to be admitted

2. **Procedures Manual** (Inventory Control & Recordkeeping System -- ICRS):
   - How merchandise will be admitted, tracked, and removed
   - Inventory control methods (lot numbers, unique identifiers)
   - Recordkeeping system details
   - Beginning balance, cumulative receipts/removals, adjustments, current balance tracking
   - Compliance with 19 CFR 146.21-146.25
   - Must specify merchandise by zone lot number or unique identifier

3. **Security Plan:**
   - Physical security of the site (fencing, access control, cameras)
   - Adequate enclosures to segregate zone from customs territory (per 19 U.S.C. 81l)
   - Personnel access controls

4. **Background Information:**
   - Officers and key personnel for background checks
   - Corporate structure and ownership

5. **Foreign Trade Zone Operator's Bond:**
   - Executed on CBP Form 301 (Customs Bond)
   - Bond conditions per 19 CFR 113.73
   - **Minimum: $50,000 per activated location** (per CBP interim guidance, August 28, 2020)
   - Bond type: Continuous bond (code 4)
   - Bond covers: duties, taxes, charges on missing/unaccounted merchandise; compliance with laws and regulations; ISF requirements ($5,000 per violation); CBP officer compensation

6. **Grantee Concurrence:**
   - Written approval from the grantee (Greater Miami Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc.)

#### Step 4.2: CBP Port Director Review

- Port Director reviews application
- Includes: site inspection, security evaluation, ICRS review, background checks
- Port Director may request modifications or additional information

#### Step 4.3: Activation Approval

- Port Director issues written decision to approve or deny
- This is the **final CBP administrative determination**
- Upon approval AND acceptance of the executed bond, the zone site is considered **activated**
- Merchandise may then be admitted to the zone

### PHASE 5: COMMENCE OPERATIONS

#### Step 5.1: Begin Admitting Merchandise

- Use CBP Form 214 for FTZ admissions
- Maintain ICRS per approved procedures manual
- Comply with all 19 CFR Part 146 requirements

#### Step 5.2: Annual Reporting

- Submit annual report to grantee by January 31 each year (for fiscal year ending December 31)
- Grantee files annual report to FTZ Board

## 8. Who Can Apply -- Eligibility Analysis

### Can Miami Alliance 3PL Apply Directly as a Grantee?

**NO.** Under 15 CFR 400.12 and 19 U.S.C. 81b:

- **Public corporations** (state, municipality, public agency) are preferred
- **Private non-profit corporations** may apply if supported by state enabling legislation
- **Private for-profit corporations** may apply ONLY if supported by a **special act of the Florida state legislature** naming the applicant corporation AND the corporation is chartered specifically for FTZ purposes

Miami Alliance 3PL is a private for-profit company and would need a **special act of the Florida legislature** to become a grantee -- this is impractical and unnecessary.

### Can Miami Alliance 3PL Apply as an Operator Under Existing FTZ 32?

**YES.** This is the recommended path:

Per 15 CFR 400.2, a **"Zone operator"** is defined as a corporation, partnership, or individual that operates within a zone site under agreement with the grantee and with CBP concurrence.

**The process:**

1. Enter into an Operator Agreement with the grantee (Greater Miami Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc.)
2. Grantee sponsors the usage-driven site designation (ASF MBM) with the FTZ Board
3. After FTZ Board approval, Miami Alliance 3PL applies to CBP for activation
4. Upon CBP activation, Miami Alliance 3PL operates as the zone operator

**Key requirement from FTZ 32 Zone Schedule:** "All entities requesting the Grantee to apply for authority to establish a usage driven/subzone site must first enter into an agreement with the Grantee governing the proposed operations at the site."

### Role of the Grantee

The grantee (Greater Miami Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc.):

- Sponsors all applications to the FTZ Board on behalf of operators
- The interested company prepares its own application; the grantee sponsors it
- Operates FTZ 32 as a public utility per 19 U.S.C. 81n
- Provides uniform treatment to all zone participants
- Charges fair and reasonable fees
- Files annual reports to the FTZ Board
- Does NOT participate in the day-to-day operations of the FTZ site

## 13. CBP Activation Requirements

### 19 CFR 146.6 -- Procedure for Activation

**Who applies:** The zone operator (Miami Alliance 3PL), or where there is no operator, the grantee.

**Application contents:**

1. Description of all zone sites covered by the application
2. Any operation to be conducted therein
3. Statement of the general character of merchandise to be admitted

**Port Director's review includes:**

1. **Background checks** on officers and key personnel
2. **Site security survey** (adequate enclosures, access controls, surveillance)
3. **Inventory control and recordkeeping system (ICRS) review**
4. **Procedures manual evaluation**
5. **Bond adequacy assessment**

**Decision:** Port Director issues written decision. This is the **final CBP administrative determination**.

### Operator Bond Requirements (19 CFR 113.73)

**Form:** CBP Form 301 (Customs Bond)
**Type:** Continuous bond
**Minimum Amount:** $50,000 per activated location (per CBP interim guidance, August 28, 2020)

**Bond Conditions:**

- **(a) Receipt, Handling, and Disposition:** Comply with all laws/regulations regarding merchandise receipt, admission, status, handling, transfer, and removal. Maintain ICRS. Penalties: value of merchandise for defaults involving non-domestic merchandise (triple for restricted/prohibited goods or alcohol); $1,000 per default not involving merchandise.
- **(b) Payment of Duties/Taxes/Charges:** Pay all duties, taxes, and charges on merchandise properly admitted but missing or unaccounted for.
- **(c) Importer Security Filing (ISF):** Comply with 19 CFR Part 149 requirements. Penalty: $5,000 per violation.
- **(d) Exoneration and Reimbursement:** Exonerate the U.S. from risk/loss; pay CBP Officer compensation as required.
- **(e) Annual Fee Payment:** Timely payment required. Late penalties escalate: 1% daily (days 1-7), 2% daily (days 8-14), 3% daily (day 15+).

**Bond review triggers:**

- Substantial alterations to activated zone area
- Significant changes in merchandise or operations
- Annual review showing liability growth >10%
- Zone violations or potential violations
- Other factors affecting operator liability

**Where to get bond:** Through a licensed surety company (annual premium typically 1-5% of bond amount, i.e., $500-$2,500 for a $50,000 bond).

### Inventory Control & Recordkeeping System (ICRS) -- 19 CFR 146.21-146.25

**Requirements:**

- Track all merchandise by zone lot number or unique identifier
- Record: beginning balance, cumulative receipts, cumulative removals, adjustments, current balance on hand (by date and quantity)
- Must be maintained at all times
- Operator is liable to CBP for defects/failures in the system
- System must be approved by the Port Director as part of activation

**Common ICRS approaches:**

- Custom-built warehouse management system (WMS) with FTZ module
- Commercial FTZ software (SAP GTS, Oracle Trade Management, Amber Road, etc.)
- Specialized FTZ-focused providers (QuestaWeb, Integration Point, etc.)
- Manual system (for small operations -- less common)

## 14. Ongoing Compliance & Operations

### Operational Requirements (15 CFR 400.41)

- Obtain all necessary federal, state, and local permits before commencing operations
- Obtain grantee approval before activation
- Obtain Port Director approval before activation
- Comply with CBP automated systems requirements

### Public Utility Obligation (19 U.S.C. 81n; 15 CFR 400.44)

- Zone must be operated as a public utility
- All rates and charges must be fair and reasonable
- Uniform treatment for all users under similar conditions
- Zone schedule must be freely available for public inspection

### Annual Reporting

- **To Grantee:** Annual report due January 31 (for fiscal year ending December 31)
- **Contents:** Certified statements of accuracy covering zone activity, merchandise volumes, employment
- **Grantee to FTZ Board:** Grantee compiles and submits annual reports

### Merchandise Status Options

When goods are admitted to the FTZ, they can have one of several statuses:

- **Privileged Foreign (PF):** Duty rate locked at time of admission (beneficial if rates are expected to rise)
- **Non-Privileged Foreign (NPF):** Duty rate determined at time of entry into customs territory (beneficial if manipulation in zone changes classification to lower duty rate)
- **Domestic (D):** U.S. goods moved into zone
- **Zone Restricted (ZR):** Goods that must be exported or destroyed

### Key Ongoing CBP Compliance

- Maintain ICRS per approved procedures manual
- Use CBP Form 214 for all admissions
- File entries when merchandise is removed from zone into customs territory
- Allow CBP access for inspection/audit at any time
- Submit to annual bond sufficiency reviews
- Maintain physical security and access controls

### Revocation/Lapse Risks

- **5-year lapse:** Grant of authority lapses if zone is not activated within 5 years of Board order (15 CFR 400.13)
- **Revocation:** Board may revoke after four months' notice for repeated willful violations (19 U.S.C. 81r)
- **CBP deactivation:** Port Director can suspend/revoke activation for non-compliance

## Appendix A: Glossary of Key FTZ Terms

| Term                     | Definition                                                                                |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Activation**           | CBP approval to operate zone procedures at a designated site                              |
| **ASF**                  | Alternative Site Framework -- streamlined procedure for designating FTZ sites             |
| **CBP**                  | U.S. Customs and Border Protection                                                        |
| **Grantee**              | Entity holding the grant of authority from the FTZ Board to establish and operate a zone  |
| **ICRS**                 | Inventory Control and Recordkeeping System -- required by CBP for all FTZ operators       |
| **Magnet Site**          | FTZ site at a port or industrial park open to multiple operators                          |
| **MBM**                  | Minor Boundary Modification -- procedure to add/modify sites under ASF                    |
| **Operator**             | Entity that operates an FTZ site under agreement with the grantee and CBP concurrence     |
| **Port Director**        | Local CBP official who approves activation and oversees zone operations                   |
| **Production Authority** | Separate FTZ Board approval required for manufacturing/substantial transformation         |
| **Subzone**              | FTZ site approved for a specific company/use outside the traditional zone boundaries      |
| **Usage-Driven Site**    | Site designated for a specific operator under the ASF framework                           |
| **Zone Schedule**        | Document listing all rates, charges, and policies for a zone (public utility requirement) |
