Wholesale 3PL — Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from factories and brands evaluating wholesale 3PL partnerships with Miami Alliance 3PL

What is wholesale 3PL?

Wholesale 3PL (third-party logistics) is an outsourced warehousing and fulfillment model designed for factories, manufacturers, and brands that sell in bulk quantities. Instead of managing your own warehouse, a wholesale 3PL partner like Miami Alliance 3PL receives your pallets, stores inventory, breaks down cases, and ships orders to retailers, distributors, marketplaces, and direct-to-consumer customers. It eliminates the overhead of leasing warehouse space, hiring staff, and managing shipping logistics — allowing brands to focus on product development and sales while the 3PL handles the physical supply chain.

How much does wholesale 3PL cost?

Wholesale 3PL pricing depends on storage volume (pallet positions), order volume, pick-pack complexity, and value-added services like kitting or EDI compliance. Miami Alliance 3PL offers transparent per-pallet storage rates, per-order fulfillment fees, and no hidden surcharges. Most wholesale brands spend between $3–8 per order for pick-pack-ship and $15–35 per pallet per month for storage. Use our online quote calculator for an instant estimate based on your specific SKU count, monthly volume, and channel mix.

Can you handle both B2B and DTC orders?

Yes. Miami Alliance 3PL operates a unified inventory model where the same stock pool serves B2B wholesale accounts, marketplace orders (Amazon, eBay, Walmart), DTC e-commerce (Shopify, WooCommerce), and retail replenishment — all from one warehouse. Each channel has its own pick-pack-ship logic, labeling requirements, and shipping rules, but inventory is shared so you never need duplicate stock pools or separate warehouse relationships for different sales channels.

Do you support EDI integration?

Yes. Miami Alliance 3PL supports all major EDI transaction sets required by U.S. retailers including EDI 850 (Purchase Orders), EDI 856 (Advance Ship Notices), and EDI 810 (Invoices). We handle EDI compliance for retailers like Walmart, Target, Costco, Amazon Vendor Central, Best Buy, and regional chains. Our team ensures ASN accuracy, GS1-128 label compliance, and routing guide adherence to prevent chargebacks and maintain vendor scorecard performance.

What’s the minimum volume?

There is no minimum volume requirement at Miami Alliance 3PL. We work with emerging brands shipping as few as one pallet per month and established manufacturers moving full truckloads weekly. Our pricing scales with your volume — the more you ship, the lower your per-unit cost. Whether you are launching your first wholesale line or expanding an existing distribution operation, we build a plan that fits your current volume and grows with your brand.

How quickly can we onboard?

Most wholesale brands complete onboarding in 1–2 weeks from discovery call to first shipment. Week one covers SKU intake, system integration (Shopify, Amazon, WooCommerce, EDI), slotting optimization, and receiving your first inventory. Week two finalizes pick-pack workflows, shipping rules per channel, and processes the first live orders. For brands with complex EDI requirements or large SKU catalogs, onboarding may extend to 3 weeks to ensure full routing guide compliance before the first retail shipment.
