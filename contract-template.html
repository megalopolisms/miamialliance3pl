Article 1 — Definitions

1.1 "Goods" means all products, merchandise, materials, and inventory delivered by or on behalf of Client to Provider's Facility for storage, handling, or processing under this Agreement.

1.2 "Facility" means Provider's warehouse located at 8780 NW 100th ST, Medley, Florida 33178, including all associated loading docks, staging areas, and storage areas.

1.3 "Services" means the warehousing, fulfillment, logistics, and related services described in Article 3 and Schedule A attached hereto.

1.4 "Rate Card" means the schedule of fees and charges for Services as set forth in Schedule B, which may be amended from time to time by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

1.5 "Service Level Agreement" or "SLA" means the performance metrics and standards set forth in Schedule C.

1.6 "Business Day" means Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays observed in the State of Florida.

1.7 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, including but not limited to pricing, customer lists, product information, trade secrets, and business strategies.

1.8 "Hazardous Materials" means any substance or material regulated under applicable federal, state, or local environmental or health and safety laws, regulations, or ordinances.

Article 2 — Term and Termination

2.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of months (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article 2.

2.2 Renewal. Following the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of months each (each a "Renewal Term"), unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

2.3 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other Party. During the notice period, both Parties shall continue to perform their respective obligations hereunder.

2.4 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; (b) becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, or has such a petition filed against it; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) ceases to conduct business in the normal course.

2.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (a) Client shall remove all Goods from the Facility within thirty (30) days at Client's expense; (b) all outstanding fees and charges shall become immediately due and payable; (c) Provider shall retain a lien on all Goods in its possession until all amounts owed are paid in full; and (d) the provisions of Articles 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 13, and 14 shall survive termination.

2.6 Transition Assistance. Upon termination, Provider shall cooperate in good faith with Client to facilitate an orderly transition of the Goods and Services to Client or its designee, provided that Client has paid all amounts due under this Agreement.

Article 4 — Pricing and Payment

4.1 Rate Card. Client shall pay Provider for Services rendered in accordance with the Rate Card attached as Schedule B. The Rate Card sets forth all applicable fees including, but not limited to, storage fees, handling fees, fulfillment fees, shipping surcharges, and any special services fees.

4.2 Invoicing. Provider shall issue invoices to Client on a basis. Invoices shall detail all charges incurred during the preceding billing period, including an itemized breakdown of services performed.

4.3 Payment Terms. All invoices shall be due and payable within days from the date of invoice. Payments shall be made in United States Dollars by ACH, wire transfer, credit card, or such other method as the Parties may agree.

4.4 Late Payment. Any amount not paid when due shall bear interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less. Client shall also be responsible for all costs of collection, including reasonable attorneys' fees.

4.5 Disputed Invoices. Client shall notify Provider of any disputed charges within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice. Undisputed amounts shall remain due and payable regardless of any dispute. The Parties agree to negotiate in good faith to resolve any disputed charges.

4.6 Rate Adjustments. Provider may adjust the Rate Card once per calendar year upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to Client. Annual rate adjustments shall not exceed the greater of: (a) five percent (5%); or (b) the percentage increase in the Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) for the Miami-Fort Lauderdale metropolitan area during the preceding twelve-month period.

4.7 Minimum Monthly Charges. Client agrees to a minimum monthly billing amount of $ (the "Monthly Minimum"). If actual charges in any month are less than the Monthly Minimum, the Monthly Minimum shall apply.

4.8 Warehouseman's Lien. Provider shall have a general lien on all Goods and documents relating to Goods in its possession for all lawful charges for storage, transportation, handling, and other services, and for all lawful claims for money advanced, plus interest, as permitted under the Florida Uniform Commercial Code (§672 et seq.).

Article 5 — Receiving and Shipping

5.1 Inbound Shipments. Client shall provide advance shipping notice (ASN) at least 48 hours prior to delivery of Goods to the Facility. ASN shall include: expected delivery date, carrier name, number of pallets/cartons, product description, and any special handling requirements.

5.2 Receiving Process. Upon receipt, Provider shall: (a) verify quantities against the ASN and bill of lading; (b) inspect Goods for visible damage; (c) photograph all received shipments; (d) update inventory records in the Customer Portal within one (1) Business Day; and (e) promptly notify Client of any discrepancies or damage.

5.3 Outbound Orders. Client shall submit shipping orders through the Customer Portal or by email. Orders received before 2:00 PM EST on a Business Day shall be processed same-day, subject to carrier pickup schedules and Service Level Agreement terms.

5.4 Carrier Selection. Unless otherwise instructed by Client, Provider shall select carriers based on cost-effectiveness and reliability. Client may specify preferred carriers, in which case Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to accommodate such preferences.

5.5 Shipping Documentation. Provider shall generate all required shipping labels, bills of lading, packing slips, and export documentation as applicable. Client is responsible for providing accurate customs and export compliance information for international shipments.

Article 6 — Inventory Management

6.1 Inventory Tracking. Provider shall maintain accurate records of all Goods received, stored, and shipped using its warehouse management system. Client shall have real-time access to inventory data through the Customer Portal.

6.2 Cycle Counts. Provider shall perform monthly cycle counts and provide results to Client. A full physical inventory shall be conducted at least once per calendar year at a mutually agreed time.

6.3 Inventory Accuracy. Provider shall maintain an inventory accuracy rate of not less than ninety-nine percent (99%) as measured by annual physical inventory. Discrepancies exceeding this threshold shall be subject to the claims process described in Article 7.

6.4 Shrinkage Allowance. Provider shall be responsible for inventory shrinkage in excess of one-half of one percent (0.5%) per annum of the total declared value of Goods stored. Shrinkage within the allowance shall be the responsibility of Client.

6.5 Reporting. Provider shall furnish Client with the following reports: (a) daily receiving and shipping reports; (b) weekly inventory status reports; (c) monthly inventory reconciliation reports; and (d) ad hoc reports as reasonably requested by Client.

Article 7 — Liability and Claims

7.1 Standard of Care. Provider shall exercise the care that a reasonably careful person would exercise under like circumstances in the handling, storage, and protection of Client's Goods. Provider shall not be liable for any loss of or damage to Goods resulting from causes beyond its reasonable control.

7.2 Liability Limit. Provider's liability for loss of or damage to Goods shall be limited to the lesser of: (a) the declared value of the Goods as stated on the applicable receipt or inventory record; or (b) $ per pound per article, or $ per occurrence, whichever is less. Client may declare a higher value by written notice and payment of applicable valuation charges.

7.3 Exclusions. Provider shall not be liable for: (a) loss or damage caused by the nature of the Goods, including inherent vice, shrinkage, or spontaneous combustion; (b) damage from atmospheric conditions including humidity, temperature changes, or condensation, unless Provider has specifically agreed to maintain controlled conditions; (c) loss or damage caused by acts of God, war, terrorism, insurrection, riot, strike, or acts of governmental authority; (d) damage caused by Client's packaging, labeling, or failure to comply with the terms of this Agreement; or (e) consequential, incidental, special, or punitive damages of any kind.

7.4 Claims Process. All claims for loss, damage, or overcharge must be submitted in writing to Provider within thirty (30) days of the date the loss, damage, or overcharge was discovered or should reasonably have been discovered. Claims not submitted within such period shall be deemed waived. Provider shall investigate and respond to all claims within thirty (30) days of receipt.

7.5 Mitigation. Each Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its damages and losses arising from any claim hereunder.

Article 8 — Insurance

8.1 Provider's Insurance. During the term of this Agreement, Provider shall maintain the following insurance coverages at its own expense:

  1. Commercial General Liability with minimum limits of $1,000,000 per occurrence and $2,000,000 aggregate;
  2. Warehouse Legal Liability with minimum limits of $ per occurrence;
  3. Workers' Compensation insurance as required by the State of Florida;
  4. Commercial Automobile Liability with minimum limits of $1,000,000 per occurrence (if providing pickup/delivery services); and
  5. Umbrella/Excess Liability with minimum limits of $2,000,000.

8.2 Client's Insurance. Client shall maintain, at its own expense: (a) all-risk property insurance on the Goods stored at the Facility for the full replacement value thereof; and (b) commercial general liability insurance with minimum limits of $1,000,000 per occurrence.

8.3 Additional Insured. Each Party shall, upon request, name the other Party as an additional insured on its commercial general liability and warehouse/property insurance policies. Each Party shall provide certificates of insurance upon request and shall provide thirty (30) days' advance written notice of any cancellation or material change in coverage.

8.4 Waiver of Subrogation. Each Party hereby waives, and shall cause its insurers to waive, all rights of subrogation against the other Party to the extent of any insurance recovery received.

Article 11 — Force Majeure

11.1 Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance of its obligations under this Agreement to the extent such failure or delay results from causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God; fire; flood; hurricane; earthquake; epidemic or pandemic; war; terrorism; riot; embargo; labor strikes or disputes (not involving the affected Party's employees); governmental actions or regulations; power failures; internet or telecommunications failures; or disruptions to transportation networks (each, a "Force Majeure Event").

11.2 The affected Party shall: (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event; (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of and resume performance; and (c) resume performance promptly upon cessation of the Force Majeure Event.

11.3 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination.

Article 12 — Dispute Resolution

12.1 Negotiation. The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement through good-faith negotiation between senior executives of each Party. If the dispute is not resolved within thirty (30) days after written notice of the dispute, either Party may proceed to mediation.

12.2 Mediation. Before commencing litigation, the Parties agree to participate in mediation administered by the American Arbitration Association or a mutually agreed mediator in Miami-Dade County, Florida. The costs of mediation shall be shared equally by the Parties.

12.3 Litigation. If mediation does not resolve the dispute within sixty (60) days, either Party may bring an action in the state or federal courts located in Miami-Dade County, Florida, and each Party hereby irrevocably consents to the jurisdiction of such courts.

12.4 Attorneys' Fees. In any action to enforce or interpret this Agreement, the prevailing Party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing Party.

Article 13 — Data Protection & Technology

13.1 Data Ownership. All data relating to Client's Goods, customers, orders, and business operations ("Client Data") shall remain the exclusive property of Client at all times. Provider acquires no ownership rights, licenses, or intellectual property interest in Client Data by virtue of storing, processing, or handling such data under this Agreement.

13.2 Customer Portal Access. Provider shall grant Client access to the Customer Portal for real-time visibility into inventory levels, order status, shipment tracking, and reporting. Provider shall maintain the Customer Portal with reasonable uptime in accordance with the SLA set forth in Schedule C. Client shall be responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of its login credentials and shall promptly notify Provider of any unauthorized access.

13.3 Data Security. Provider shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect Client Data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. Such safeguards shall include, at a minimum: (a) access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis; (b) encryption of Client Data in transit and at rest; (c) regular security assessments and vulnerability testing; and (d) incident response procedures.

13.4 Data Breach Notification. In the event of any actual or reasonably suspected unauthorized access to or disclosure of Client Data ("Data Breach"), Provider shall: (a) notify Client in writing within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of the Data Breach; (b) cooperate with Client in investigating and remediating the Data Breach; (c) take all reasonable steps to mitigate the effects of the Data Breach; and (d) provide Client with such information as Client may reasonably request regarding the nature, scope, and impact of the Data Breach.

13.5 Data Privacy Compliance. Each Party shall comply with all applicable data privacy and protection laws, including but not limited to the Florida Information Protection Act (FIPA), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) to the extent applicable, and any other applicable state, federal, or international data protection regulations. Provider shall not sell, share, or use Client Data for any purpose other than performing the Services under this Agreement.

13.6 Data Return and Deletion. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Provider shall, at Client's election: (a) return all Client Data in a commercially standard and reasonably usable format within thirty (30) days; or (b) securely delete or destroy all Client Data and certify such deletion in writing. Provider may retain copies of Client Data only to the extent required by applicable law or for legitimate record-keeping purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

13.7 System Integration. Where the Parties agree to integrate their respective technology systems (including but not limited to ERP, OMS, or e-commerce platforms), such integrations shall be documented in a written technical specification, and each Party shall bear its own integration costs unless otherwise agreed. Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain compatibility with Client's systems.

13.8 Intellectual Property. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to transfer any intellectual property rights from one Party to the other. Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in and to its own intellectual property, including but not limited to software, trademarks, trade secrets, and proprietary processes.
