TWIC Port Pickup & Delivery FAQ

Answers for importers, exporters, and logistics teams planning secure South Florida port moves.

What does TWIC mean here?

TWIC means Transportation Worker Identification Credential. We use the more precise public wording TWIC-credentialed and TSA-vetted because the credential is issued through the TSA for secure maritime access.

Do you support pickup and drop-off inside PortMiami and Port Everglades?

Yes. Miami Alliance 3PL supports secure pickup and drop-off inside both ports, then ties the move into receiving, staging, and next-step warehouse execution when needed.

Can you help with import and export moves?

Yes. This page is built for inbound import pickups, outbound export drop-offs, rework staging, and freight that needs a reliable South Florida warehouse handoff after the port move.

How quickly can you receive freight after pickup?

That depends on terminal release timing, appointment windows, and cargo readiness, but the workflow is designed to move freight from secure port access into warehouse receiving as quickly as possible.

Do you coordinate with brokers and freight forwarders?

Yes. We work with brokers, forwarders, dispatchers, and warehouse teams so the move has a single operating thread instead of fragmented status updates.

What should I send to start a port move?

Send the port, terminal or release details, cargo profile, timing requirements, and the contacts involved. We can then align the pickup or drop-off with the right receiving or outbound plan.
