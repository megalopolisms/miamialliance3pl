Why Miami Is a Top 3PL Hub

Before diving into individual providers, it is worth understanding why so many fulfillment centers have clustered in the Miami-Dade and Broward County corridors — particularly in the industrial zones of Medley, Doral, Hialeah, and the areas surrounding Miami International Airport.

PortMiami is the largest container port in Florida and among the top ten in the United States. It handles over 1.1 million TEUs (twenty-foot equivalent units) annually, with direct shipping routes to over 100 countries. For brands importing goods from Asia, Europe, or Latin America, having a 3PL within miles of PortMiami eliminates costly drayage and inland freight.

Miami International Airport (MIA) is the single largest gateway for air freight between the United States and Latin America — handling roughly 84% of all air cargo to and from South America and 80% of air imports and exports to Central America and the Caribbean. If your supply chain involves LATAM, there is no better location.

Beyond infrastructure, Miami offers a bilingual workforce (over 70% of the metro area speaks Spanish), proximity to Free Trade Zones and bonded warehouses, a business-friendly tax environment with no state income tax, and 2-day ground shipping coverage to roughly 100 million consumers in the Southeast United States. The combination of port access, air freight capacity, LATAM connectivity, and domestic shipping reach makes Miami a uniquely versatile location for e-commerce fulfillment.

APS Fulfillment is one of the more established 3PL operations in South Florida, with over two decades of experience in the fulfillment space. 3. Phase V Fulfillment

❄️
Location: South Florida
Best For: Apparel, beauty, and CBD brands needing temperature control
Notable: 70,000+ sqft, temperature-controlled storage

Phase V Fulfillment operates a 70,000+ square foot facility with temperature-controlled storage, which is a critical differentiator in South Florida where warehouse temperatures can exceed 100°F in summer. For brands selling products that degrade in heat — cosmetics, skincare, candles, chocolate, supplements, and CBD products — temperature control is not optional. It is a requirement.

Phase V has invested heavily in their software platform, offering real-time inventory dashboards, order tracking, and analytics. They also maintain industry-specific landing pages for apparel, beauty, and CBD fulfillment, which suggests deep category expertise and purpose-built workflows for these verticals. Their team understands the unique handling requirements for garments (folding, hanging, poly bagging), beauty products (fragile packaging, lot tracking), and CBD (compliance labeling, age-gated shipping restrictions).

Phase V is a good fit for brands in temperature-sensitive categories that need a mid-size provider with strong technology. Brands selling standard dry goods or those with very high volume may find other options on this list more cost-effective.

4. Stockmatic

🔒
Location: Miami area
Best For: Importers and brands selling to big box retailers (Costco, Target, TJX)
Notable: Bonded warehouse / Foreign Trade Zone, EDI integrations

Stockmatic occupies a distinct niche in the Miami 3PL market: they operate a bonded warehouse with Foreign Trade Zone (FTZ) access. This is significant for brands that import goods and want to defer, reduce, or eliminate customs duties. In an FTZ, imported goods can be stored, assembled, manufactured, or re-exported without paying U.S. customs duties until the goods formally enter U.S. commerce. For high-value imports, the cash flow benefit is substantial.

Stockmatic also provides Amazon FBA prep services and, critically, EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) integrations with major retailers including Costco, Target, TJX Companies (TJ Maxx, Marshalls, HomeGoods), and other big box chains. EDI compliance is a non-negotiable requirement for selling to these retailers, and getting it wrong results in chargebacks and lost accounts. Having a 3PL that handles EDI natively saves brands from building and maintaining these integrations themselves.

Stockmatic is the strongest option for brands that import products internationally and distribute to major retail accounts. Brands that sell exclusively DTC or on Amazon and do not need FTZ or EDI capabilities may not get full value from Stockmatic's specialized infrastructure.

NXTPoint Logistics operates a 132,000 square foot warehouse in the Miami market and offers services that go well beyond standard pick-and-pack fulfillment. Their operation includes freight forwarding, white glove delivery, and enterprise B2B distribution — services that most e-commerce-focused 3PLs do not provide. If you ship large, heavy, or high-value items that require special handling, liftgate delivery, room-of-choice placement, or assembly, NXTPoint has the infrastructure for it.

NXTPoint runs on Korber WMS (formerly HighJump), which is an enterprise-grade warehouse management system used by some of the largest logistics operations in the world. This signals a level of operational sophistication and scalability that smaller WMS platforms cannot match. Korber supports complex workflows including wave planning, slotting optimization, labor management, and multi-client operations.

The combination of large-footprint warehousing, freight forwarding, white glove services, and enterprise WMS makes NXTPoint a strong fit for brands selling furniture, appliances, fitness equipment, and other oversized or high-value products. Standard small-parcel e-commerce brands may find the service level and pricing oriented toward larger, more complex operations.

Taylored's historical specialty is apparel fulfillment. They handle garment-on-hanger (GOH) storage, pressing, steaming, ticketing, and re-tagging at scale. For fashion brands that sell both wholesale (B2B to department stores and boutiques) and direct-to-consumer (DTC through their own website), Taylored offers omnichannel distribution from a single inventory pool. This means the same warehouse fulfills retail purchase orders with EDI compliance and individual e-commerce orders with branded packaging, without maintaining separate inventory.

The scale and enterprise orientation of Taylored Services makes them best suited for high-volume brands doing tens of thousands of orders per month. Smaller e-commerce brands or those outside the apparel vertical will likely find more attentive service and more appropriate pricing from mid-size providers. But for brands at scale, particularly in fashion and apparel, Taylored is a serious contender.

How to Choose the Right Miami 3PL

With seven strong options in the Miami market, the right choice depends on your specific situation. Here are the key criteria to evaluate:

Order Volume

Be honest about your current volume and realistic about growth projections. If you ship 100-500 orders per month, a provider with low minimums (like Miami Alliance's $1,000/mo) will be more cost-effective than one designed for 10,000+ orders. Conversely, if you are doing 50,000 orders per month, you need the infrastructure of a Taylored Services or NXTPoint.

Product Type

Temperature-sensitive products need Phase V. Heavy or oversized items point to NXTPoint. Apparel at scale fits Taylored Services. Imported goods for retail accounts need Stockmatic's FTZ and EDI. General e-commerce SKUs work well with Miami Alliance or APS.

Budget and Pricing Model

Ask for an all-in quote, not just a per-order price. Factor in storage fees, receiving fees, account management fees, integration fees, and any minimums. A 3PL that charges $2.50 per order but has a $3,000 monthly minimum is more expensive than one charging $3.50 per order with a $1,000 minimum if you only ship 500 orders per month.

Platform Integrations

Confirm your e-commerce platform is supported before going further. If you sell on Shopify plus Amazon plus Walmart Marketplace, your 3PL needs to sync inventory across all three in real time. APS has the broadest integration library (106+ platforms). Other providers may handle the major platforms but not niche marketplaces.

Contract Terms

Some providers require 6- or 12-month commitments. Others operate month-to-month. For brands outsourcing fulfillment for the first time, a no-contract arrangement dramatically reduces risk. You can test the relationship for 60-90 days before deciding whether to commit long-term.

International Needs

If you import products, export to LATAM, or serve international customers, prioritize 3PLs with cross-border expertise. Miami Alliance and iLogistics USA specialize in LATAM logistics. Stockmatic offers FTZ benefits for importers. Not every Miami 3PL handles international shipping with the same depth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a 3PL warehouse cost in Miami?

3PL warehouse costs in Miami vary by provider and service level. Storage typically runs $10 to $20 per pallet per month. Pick-and-pack fees range from $1.50 to $5.00 per order depending on complexity and item count. Receiving fees are usually $25 to $50 per pallet or container. Some providers charge monthly minimums of $1,000 to $5,000, while others like Miami Alliance 3PL have a low $1,000/mo minimum spend. Always request an all-in quote that includes storage, handling, shipping, and any technology or account management fees — the per-order price alone does not tell the full story.

What's the minimum order volume for a Miami 3PL?

Minimum order volume requirements vary widely across Miami 3PL providers. Large national providers like Taylored Services and NXTPoint Logistics typically require hundreds or thousands of orders per month to justify onboarding and account management costs. Mid-size providers like APS Fulfillment and Phase V may have moderate minimums. Some providers, including Miami Alliance 3PL, have a low $1,000/mo minimum spend, making them accessible to startups and small brands shipping as few as 50 orders per month. If minimums are a concern, ask about them upfront before investing time in the onboarding conversation.

Can I use multiple 3PL providers?

Yes, and many brands do. Common reasons include geographic coverage (one warehouse on the East Coast, one on the West Coast to reduce transit times and shipping costs), specialization (one 3PL for standard e-commerce fulfillment, another for Amazon FBA prep or retail distribution), and risk mitigation (avoiding a single point of failure if one facility has issues). The tradeoff is increased complexity in inventory management, higher technology integration costs, and split purchasing power on carrier rates. Most brands start with a single 3PL and add a second only when they exceed 5,000 orders per month or need multi-region coverage.

How long does it take to onboard with a Miami 3PL?

Onboarding timelines range from one week to six weeks depending on the provider and the complexity of your operation. A simple setup with a single sales channel, standard products, and a supported e-commerce platform can be live in 5 to 10 business days. Complex integrations involving multiple platforms, custom packaging, kitting, or international shipping may take 3 to 6 weeks. Key factors that affect timeline include e-commerce platform integration setup, custom packaging or labeling requirements, the number of SKUs to catalog and receive, and whether you need specialized storage like temperature control or bonded warehousing.
