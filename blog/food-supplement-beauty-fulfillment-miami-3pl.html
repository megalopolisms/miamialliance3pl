Industry-Specific Fulfillment Requirements

Food, supplements, and beauty products are not commodities you can throw on a shelf and ship when ordered. Each category has specific handling requirements that a generic warehouse often cannot meet. If your 3PL does not understand these requirements, the consequences range from spoiled inventory and customer complaints to FDA warning letters and product recalls.

Food and Beverage

If you sell packaged food products online — whether snack bars, sauces, coffee, specialty candy, or meal kits — your fulfillment partner needs to meet a specific set of requirements:

  • FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) awareness. While a 3PL storing finished, packaged food typically does not need its own FDA registration, it must follow Good Manufacturing Practices (GMPs) for food storage. This means clean facilities, pest control programs, and separation from non-food products that could contaminate inventory.
  • Lot tracking and traceability. Every food product must be traceable from receipt to shipment at the lot level. If a recall occurs, you need to know exactly which customers received affected lot numbers. Your 3PL's WMS must support lot-level inventory tracking, not just SKU-level.
  • FEFO inventory rotation. Food products must ship on a First Expired, First Out (FEFO) basis, not standard FIFO. The WMS must track expiration dates at the lot level and automatically pick the nearest-to-expiration inventory first.
  • Temperature control. Even shelf-stable food products have temperature ranges. Chocolate melts above 80°F. Gummies can deform in high heat. In Miami's subtropical climate, a warehouse without reliable climate control will damage your inventory between May and October.
  • Allergen segregation. If your product line includes items with common allergens (peanuts, tree nuts, dairy, gluten), your warehouse needs protocols to prevent cross-contamination during storage and packing. This may require dedicated storage zones and cleaning procedures between picking different product lines.

Dietary Supplements and Nutraceuticals

The supplement industry has exploded in the DTC e-commerce channel, but it is also one of the most scrutinized by regulators. Here is what a supplement brand needs from its fulfillment partner:

  • 21 CFR Part 111 awareness. Supplement manufacturers must follow Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) under FDA regulations. While the 3PL is not the manufacturer, it must maintain conditions consistent with cGMP for storage and handling — including temperature control, humidity management, pest control, and clean handling procedures.
  • Lot and batch tracking. Like food, supplements require full lot traceability. If the FDA issues a recall or your manufacturer identifies a quality issue with a specific batch, your 3PL must be able to quarantine affected inventory and identify every order that shipped from that lot.
  • Expiration date management. Supplements have expiration or best-by dates that determine sellability. A WMS with FEFO rotation prevents shipping expired product and helps you manage inventory approaching expiration for promotional clearance or destruction.
  • Controlled environment storage. Probiotics, omega-3 oils, vitamin D liquids, and many other supplement formats are sensitive to heat, light, and humidity. Your warehouse needs to maintain consistent controlled room temperature (59-77°F) and potentially offer refrigerated zones for specific products.
  • Secure storage. Some supplement ingredients have significant value on the secondary market. Your 3PL should have security measures including camera surveillance, controlled access, and inventory reconciliation to prevent shrinkage.

Beauty and Cosmetics

Beauty brands face a unique combination of regulatory and operational challenges in fulfillment:

  • Fragile product handling. Glass bottles, compact mirrors, pressed powder palettes, and liquid products in pump bottles are all damage-prone. Your 3PL needs trained pickers who understand that a beauty product is not a book — it requires careful handling and appropriate protective packaging for every shipment.
  • Temperature sensitivity. Lipsticks, creams, serums, and liquid foundations can separate, melt, or change consistency when exposed to temperature extremes. This is not a theoretical risk in Miami — it is a real operational challenge that requires consistent climate control in both storage and the packing area.
  • Batch and lot tracking. Cosmetics must be traceable to the manufacturing batch. The FDA can and does issue recalls for cosmetics with contamination or mislabeling issues. Your 3PL must track which batch went into which shipment.
  • Kitting and subscription fulfillment. Many beauty brands sell subscription boxes, curated sets, or sampler kits. This requires kitting capabilities — assembling multiple individual products into a single shipment with specific presentation requirements (tissue paper, branded inserts, specific product arrangement).
  • High SKU count management. A typical beauty brand may have 50-200 active SKUs across shades, sizes, and product categories. Your 3PL needs a WMS that handles high SKU counts efficiently without picking errors. A wrong shade of foundation shipped is a guaranteed return.
📊
The Cost of Getting It Wrong: A single lot-tracking failure that results in shipping recalled product can cost a small brand $50,000-$200,000 in recall expenses, legal liability, and customer trust damage. An FDA warning letter for improper storage conditions is public record and can destroy your retail relationships overnight. These are not hypothetical risks — the FDA issued over 200 warning letters to food and supplement companies in 2025 alone, many related to storage and distribution failures.

What to Look for in a 3PL Partner for Regulated Products

Not every 3PL is equipped to handle food, supplements, or beauty products. When evaluating partners, ask these specific questions and look for these capabilities:

1

Lot-Level Inventory Tracking in the WMS

This is non-negotiable. Ask the 3PL to demonstrate lot-level tracking in their warehouse management system. Can you see which lot numbers are in stock? Can you see which lot shipped to which customer? Can you quarantine a specific lot remotely? If the answer to any of these is no, walk away. SKU-level tracking is not enough for regulated products.

2

FEFO Inventory Rotation

Ask how the WMS handles expiration dates. Does it enforce First Expired, First Out picking? Does it alert you when inventory is approaching expiration (30, 60, 90 days out)? Can it block picking for expired inventory automatically? A WMS that supports FEFO rotation at the lot level is essential for minimizing waste and ensuring customers receive product with maximum remaining shelf life.

3

Temperature Monitoring and Documentation

Ask to see temperature monitoring logs. Does the warehouse have temperature sensors in storage areas? Are readings logged automatically or manually? What happens if temperature goes out of range — is there an alert system? For products that require controlled room temperature (59-77°F), you need documented proof that conditions are maintained. Retailers like Whole Foods and Amazon may require temperature logs as part of vendor qualification.

4

Pest Control Program

A warehouse storing food or cosmetic products must have a documented pest control program with regular inspections by a licensed pest control operator. Ask for documentation. In South Florida, where pest pressure is year-round, this is not optional. Look for integrated pest management (IPM) with monitoring stations, treatment records, and corrective action logs.

5

Clean Facility with Segregated Storage

Tour the warehouse. Is it clean? Are food products stored separately from chemicals, cleaning supplies, or automotive parts? Is there a dedicated area for allergen-containing products? A 3PL that stores your organic protein bars next to cases of motor oil is not a partner for regulated products. Look for clear segregation, clean floors, organized racking, and professional housekeeping.

6

Kitting and Custom Packaging Capabilities

If you sell subscription boxes, variety packs, gift sets, or bundled products, your 3PL needs kitting capabilities. This means the ability to assemble multiple individual products into a single package according to your specifications, including branded inserts, tissue paper, specific product arrangement, and custom packaging. Ask to see examples of kitting work they have done for other clients.

7

Recall Readiness

Ask: "If we need to recall a specific lot number, how fast can you quarantine affected inventory and provide a list of every order that shipped from that lot?" If the answer is not measured in hours, the 3PL is not equipped for regulated products. Recall speed is the ultimate test of a 3PL's lot tracking capability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a 3PL need FDA registration to handle food or supplements?

It depends on the activities performed. Warehouses that only store and distribute finished, packaged food or dietary supplements generally do not need their own FDA registration, as the product manufacturer holds the registration. However, if the 3PL performs any manufacturing, processing, packing, or labeling activities, FDA registration may be required. A 3PL that handles food, supplements, or cosmetics should follow Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) and maintain detailed lot tracking and FIFO/FEFO inventory rotation regardless of registration status.

What is FEFO inventory management and why does it matter for perishable products?

FEFO stands for First Expired, First Out. Unlike standard FIFO (First In, First Out) which ships the oldest received inventory first, FEFO prioritizes items with the nearest expiration date regardless of when they were received. This is critical for food, supplements, and beauty products because it minimizes waste from expired stock, ensures customers receive products with maximum remaining shelf life, reduces the risk of shipping expired or near-expired products, and helps maintain compliance with retailer requirements for minimum shelf life on receipt.

Can a Miami 3PL help me export food or beauty products to Latin America?

Yes. Miami is the primary U.S. gateway to Latin American markets, and a Miami-based 3PL can support your LATAM expansion by coordinating export documentation and customs clearance, ensuring products meet destination country labeling requirements, managing temperature-controlled shipments through PortMiami or MIA, and staging inventory for multiple LATAM markets from a single warehouse. Many food, supplement, and beauty brands use Miami as their hub for both domestic U.S. fulfillment and LATAM distribution.

What temperature range should a warehouse maintain for supplements and beauty products?

Most dietary supplements and beauty products should be stored between 59-77°F (15-25°C), which is considered controlled room temperature. Some products, particularly probiotics, certain serums, and chocolate-based items, may require refrigerated storage at 36-46°F. In Miami's subtropical climate, a 3PL must have robust climate control systems, not just air conditioning. Look for a warehouse with temperature monitoring logs, backup power for climate systems, and the ability to maintain separate temperature zones for products with different storage requirements.
