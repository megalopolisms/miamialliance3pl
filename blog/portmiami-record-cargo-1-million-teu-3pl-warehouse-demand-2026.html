PortMiami just posted its fiscal year 2025 numbers: 1,115,058 TEUs — a 2.35% increase over the prior year and the 11th consecutive year exceeding one million TEUs. For importers, e-commerce sellers, and logistics operators in South Florida, this isn't just a port statistic. It's a warehouse capacity signal.PortMiami acaba de publicar sus números del año fiscal 2025: 1,115,058 TEUs — un aumento del 2.35% sobre el año anterior y el 11° año consecutivo superando un millón de TEUs. PortMiami's cargo growth has been remarkably consistent. While other U.S. ports saw volume declines in early 2026 — with national import forecasts showing a 2% decrease in TEU for H1 2026 — Miami's Latin American trade corridors continue to grow. The port is America's closest deepwater port to the Panama Canal, and Miami International Airport handles more international freight than any other U.S. airport.El crecimiento de carga de PortMiami ha sido notablemente consistente. Mientras otros puertos de EE.UU. vieron caídas de volumen a principios de 2026, los corredores comerciales de Miami con América Latina continúan creciendo. El puerto es el puerto de aguas profundas más cercano de Estados Unidos al Canal de Panamá.

Infrastructure Investments Driving CapacityInversiones en Infraestructura Impulsando la Capacidad

PortMiami isn't just handling more cargo — it's building the infrastructure to handle even more. The completion of phase two of the Electric Rubber-Tired Gantry Crane (eRTG) Project brings the total number of eRTGs in the South Florida Container Terminal to 18. These cranes allow for higher stacking of containers and more efficient yard operations, which means faster container throughput and shorter dwell times for importers.PortMiami no solo está manejando más carga — está construyendo la infraestructura para manejar aún más. La finalización de la fase dos del Proyecto de Grúas Pórtico de Neumáticos Eléctricas (eRTG) eleva el total a 18 grúas en la Terminal de Contenedores del Sur de Florida.

For 3PL operators, this is a green light: faster port throughput means more containers moving into warehouses faster, which means you need a 3PL partner with available space and the systems to handle surge volume.Para operadores 3PL, esto es luz verde: un mayor rendimiento portuario significa más contenedores moviéndose a los almacenes más rápido, lo que significa que necesita un socio 3PL con espacio disponible y sistemas para manejar volumen en picos.

What This Means for Warehouse Demand in Medley & DoralQué Significa Esto para la Demanda de Almacenes en Medley y Doral

South Florida's industrial real estate is already one of the tightest markets in the country. Vacancy rates in Miami-Dade's key logistics submarkets — Medley, Doral, and Hialeah Gardens — hover between 6% and 7%. Rents have climbed relentlessly for a decade, and geography severely constrains new supply. You can't build new warehouse space where there's no land.El mercado inmobiliario industrial del sur de Florida ya es uno de los más ajustados del país. Las tasas de disponibilidad en los submercados logísticos clave de Miami-Dade — Medley, Doral e Hialeah Gardens — oscilan entre 6% y 7%. Los alquileres han subido implacablemente durante una década.

When you combine record port cargo with tight warehouse supply, the math is clear: warehouse space near PortMiami is going to get more expensive and harder to find. The Cargo Mix Is Shifting — And That MattersLa Mezcla de Carga Está Cambiando — Y Eso Importa

It's not just volume that's growing — the type of cargo coming through PortMiami is evolving. The mix is shifting toward intermediate and capital goods: machinery, parts, plastics, and metals. This reflects the nearshoring trend, as Latin American factories integrate into North American supply chains. For 3PLs, this means warehousing isn't just about storing consumer goods anymore — it's about handling industrial components, B2B inventory, and cross-border supply chain staging.No solo el volumen está creciendo — el tipo de carga que pasa por PortMiami está evolucionando. La mezcla se inclina hacia bienes intermedios y de capital: maquinaria, partes, plásticos y metales. Esto refleja la tendencia de nearshoring.
